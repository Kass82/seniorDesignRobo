|||||||||| better microcontroller board ||||||

raspberry pico 2 (2015?)
- specs: https://www.raspberrypi.com/products/raspberry-pi-pico-2/
- available for as low as $5, but kinda difficult to find, jk you can find it at microcenter
- 150 MHz (pretty low compared to teensy 4.1)
- 11n wirless Lan and bluetooth 5.2
- offers better documentation



teensy 4.1 (2021?)
- specs: https://www.pjrc.com/store/teensy41.html 
- on amazon for $35-50
- 600 MHz (how fast the processor's clock is running | more work per second)
- 55 i/o pins, more flexible than pico 2
- much faster than pico 2
- larger ram, more room for heavy tasks tasks


raspberry pico 2 built for smaller projects, while teensy 4.1 can handle bigger/heavier projects.


||||| jetson orin nano ||||||

- small but powerful supercomputer, tiny nvda gpu built into embedded model
- real-time ai data: SLAM, autonomous navigation, robot perception, image/object detection
- simply use a usb cable to link the teensy and jetson orin nano
- guide: https://developer.nvidia.com/embedded/learn/get-started-jetson-orin-nano-devkit
- specs: https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/autonomous-machines/embedded-systems/jetson-orin/nano-super-developer-kit/
